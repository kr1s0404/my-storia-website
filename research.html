We're looking for parents and caregivers to participate in a research study exploring how personalized social stories can support autistic children.

About the Study

This research is part of a project at the University of Washington Bothell, conducted through the IDEA Lab. We are investigating how LLM-powered, personalized social stories can help autistic children better prepare for and navigate everyday routines and social situations.

Social stories, developed by Carol Gray, are a well-established intervention used by families, educators, and therapists worldwide. Mystoria brings this methodology into a modern, accessible app — and we need your help to evaluate its effectiveness and usability.

Who Can Participate

We are looking for participants who meet the following criteria:

Parents or Caregivers

Of a child (ages 3-12) on the autism spectrum or with related social communication needs.

iOS Device

Access to an iPhone or iPad running iOS 17.6 or later.

English Speaking

Comfortable reading and responding in English.

Available for Feedback

Willing to use the app and provide feedback through a short survey or interview.

What's Involved

Participation is straightforward and designed to fit into your existing routine:

  • Download and use Mystoria — Create social stories for your child around real-life situations they encounter.
  • Use the app for a trial period — We'll ask you to use the app over a period of approximately 2 weeks.
  • Share your experience — Complete a brief survey about your experience, the app's usability, and whether the stories were helpful. Optionally, participate in a short interview (15-20 minutes).

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without penalty.

Privacy and Data Protection

Your privacy and your child's privacy are our top priority.

  • All responses are confidential and will be de-identified in any publications or presentations.
  • We do not collect your child's personal data through the study — only your feedback about the app experience.
  • Data is stored securely and accessible only to the research team.
  • This study has been reviewed by the University of Washington IRB.
